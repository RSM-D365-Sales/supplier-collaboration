1Overview & Accessing the Admin Console

The Supplier Collaboration Portal lets your procurement team generate secure, per-vendor quotation links without any vendor login. Vendors receive a URL, fill in their prices, and submit — the response is stored and can be written back to Dynamics 365.

  1. Open a browser and navigate to the admin console:
    https://portal.rsmd365.com/supplier-collaboration/admin

  2. You will see the Portal Slots page. The portal supports up to 5 simultaneous active RFQs, one per slot.

  3. Each slot displays its current status: ● Not configured, ● Active, or ● Closed.

2Opening a Portal Slot to Configure

Unconfigured slots have a Configure button. Active slots have an Edit button that re-opens the same form without resetting vendor links.

  1. Locate an unconfigured slot (e.g. Portal 2) and click the ⚙ Configure button on the right.

  2. The slot expands to reveal the configuration form. You will see sections for: Portal Label, RFQ Number, Buyer / Company, Line Items, and Vendors.

3Entering the RFQ Number & Fetching from D365

The portal can pull the RFQ header, line items, buyer details, and invited vendors directly from Dynamics 365 with a single click — no manual data entry required.

  1. In the Portal Label field, type a friendly name for this slot (e.g. Portal 1). This is only visible in the admin console.

  2. In the RFQ Number * field, type the D365 RFQ case number exactly as it appears in Dynamics (e.g. 000460).

  3. Click the 🔍 Fetch from D365 button to the right of the RFQ Number field.

  4. The system queries Dynamics 365 and automatically populates all fields below — Company Name, Buyer Name, Buyer Email, Dates, Instructions, and all Line Items.

4Reviewing & Editing Buyer / Company Information

After the D365 fetch, all buyer fields are pre-filled. Review and correct any values before activating — these appear on the vendor-facing portal and in the invitation email.
Field Source Description
Company Name Auto from D365 Displayed in the portal header and email. Shown to the vendor as the buying organisation.
Buyer Name Auto from D365 The requester name from the RFQ header. Shown as the contact for queries.
Buyer Email * Auto from D365 Shown as a clickable mailto link on the vendor portal. Vendors click this to ask questions.
Buyer Phone Manual Optional. Displayed next to the buyer name if provided.
Entry Date Auto from D365 The RFQ delivery/entry date from D365.
Expiration Date * Auto from D365 The submission deadline shown prominently in an amber banner on the vendor portal.
Instructions Auto from D365 The RFQ title/description. Shown in italics on the vendor response page.
⚠️ All fields are editable even after the D365 fetch. Changes here only affect what the vendor sees in the portal — they do not write back to D365.

5Reviewing Line Items

Line items are pulled directly from D365 when you click Fetch from D365. Each row represents one RFQ line. Vendors will see these items and fill in their pricing.

  1. Scroll to the LINE ITEMS section. All lines from the D365 RFQ are listed with their Item ID, Description, Quantity, Unit, and Lead Days.

  2. Review the items. You can edit any field by clicking directly into the row.

  3. To add an extra line manually, click ＋ Add Item in the top-right of the section.

  4. To remove a line, click the 🗑 delete icon at the end of the row.

6Adding Vendors and Importing from D365

Each vendor requires a D365 Vendor Account Number. Clicking the lookup button imports the vendor's name and email directly from Dynamics — no manual typing needed.

  1. Scroll to the VENDORS section at the bottom of the form.

  2. Click ＋ Add Vendor to add a new vendor row. A row appears with three fields: D365 Vendor ID, Vendor Name, and Email.

  3. Type the D365 Vendor Account Number into the D365 Vendor ID field (e.g. V300475).

  4. Click the 👤 lookup icon (person-search button) to the right of the Vendor ID field.

  5. The system queries D365 and auto-populates the Vendor Name and Email fields instantly.

  6. Repeat steps 2–5 for each additional vendor. There is no limit on the number of vendors per slot.

7Activating the Portal

Clicking Activate Portal generates one secure vendor link per vendor, saves the slot, and sets the slot status to Active. No emails are sent automatically — links are shared manually.

  1. Review everything one last time: RFQ number, buyer details, line items, and vendor list.

  2. Click the ✈ Activate Portal button in the bottom-right of the form.

  3. The form closes and the slot card updates to show a green dot and the number of vendors configured (e.g. RFQ 000460 · 3 vendor(s)).

  4. The slot is now live. Click the slot header to expand and see the Vendor Responses panel.

8Viewing Vendor Responses & Sharing Links

The expanded slot shows each vendor's response status in real time, plus two actions per vendor: preview their invitation email, and open their portal directly.

  1. Click the slot card header to expand it. The VENDOR RESPONSES panel shows one row per vendor.

  2. Each row shows: Vendor Name, Email, a status badge (🕐 pending, ✅ replied, ✖ declined, or — no-reply), plus two action buttons.

  3. Click the ✉ Email button to open a new browser tab showing a preview of the invitation email that would be sent to that vendor — including their unique portal link, RFQ details, expiry date, and buyer contact.

  4. Click the ↗ external link icon to open that vendor's portal page directly in a new tab — useful for demonstrating the vendor experience or checking data.

  5. Copy the portal URL from the browser address bar and send it to the vendor manually (email, Teams, etc.).

9The Vendor Experience

When a vendor opens their unique link, they see a clean, branded response form. No login is required. The link is valid for 30 days and can only be used by the intended vendor.

  1. The vendor sees a header bar with the buying company name, their vendor name and account ID, and a Secure link badge confirming the link is for them specifically.

  2. An amber deadline banner shows the submission deadline and buyer contact details (clickable email link).

  3. Under the Response tab, the vendor selects one of three response buttons:
    ✓ Replied   ✖ Declined   — No Reply

  4. The vendor fills in Quote Valid Days and Quote Dated fields at the top right.

  5. In the line items table, the vendor enters a Lead Time (days) and Quoted Unit Price for each item. The Extended Price calculates automatically.

  6. If the vendor cannot supply a specific item, they tick the Reject item checkbox at the end of that row. The row turns red, inputs are disabled, and that line contributes zero to the total.

  7. The vendor can attach documents per line item or for the RFQ overall using the Attach Documents tab.

  8. The vendor adds any Additional Notes / Comments at the bottom and clicks 💾 Update to submit.

  9. A confirmation modal appears. The vendor can re-open the same link and update their response any time before the deadline.
